How the Token Economics Work
The mechanics driving dilution, explained from scratch.

The Big Picture

Bittensor has 128 "subnets" — competing AI projects. Each day, 3,600 new TAO (~$1M/day) are minted and distributed based on staking flows. Templar gets 6.86% (highest share) — ~247 TAO/day (~$71K). This is how GPU miners get paid. There's no revenue or customers — everything is funded by minting new tokens.

Two Layers of Dilution

Layer 1: TAO (network level)

3,600 new TAO/day across all subnets. 21M max, ~8.9M circulating. ~13% annual inflation (was 26%+ before Dec '25 halving).

Layer 2: Alpha (subnet level)

Each subnet emits its own token. Templar's alpha: 21M max, 4.22M circulating. New alpha goes to miners, validators, and the subnet creator. This is the supply growth in the charts.

What Does TAO Flowing Into the Pool Do?

Templar's alpha trades in an AMM liquidity pool against TAO. ~247 TAO/day flows in from emissions. This cuts both ways:

Bullish: Mechanical price support

More TAO in the pool's reserves pushes the alpha price up (price = TAO in pool / alpha in pool). It's like a daily cash injection. This is why market cap has grown despite constant selling.

Bearish: It's inflation, not real demand

That 247 TAO/day is newly minted — not customer revenue. If TAO's price drops (because TAO itself is diluting), the dollar value of the injection drops too. Tokens backing tokens with no external revenue anchor.

The tiebreaker: Staking flows

Since Nov '25, emission share is based on net staking inflows. More people stake into Templar = bigger share. The 6.86% isn't fixed — it's the market voting Templar most valuable. Reflexive: works great going up, unwinds going down.

Covenant-72B: What They Built & What It Cost

On March 10, 2026, Templar completed the largest permissionless decentralized AI training run in history:

  • No data center. ~20 independent contributors worldwide, each with 8 NVIDIA B200 GPUs. 70 unique participants total.
  • No whitelist. Anyone with hardware could join or leave freely.
  • Commodity internet. SparseLoCo compression (146x) made bandwidth a non-issue. 94.5% compute utilization.

Endorsed by Jensen Huang (NVIDIA CEO), Chamath Palihapitiya, and Jack Clark (Anthropic co-founder).

Scaling, Risks & Outlook

The GPU Scaling Wall

Each Covenant peer needed 8 NVIDIA B200 GPUs (~$200-300K). Bigger models need bigger machines — and fewer people have them:
