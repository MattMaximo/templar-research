How Templar Emissions Work
If you're not deep in Bittensor, this explains the mechanics driving dilution.

Bittensor has 128 "subnets" — competing AI projects. Each day, 3,600 new TAO (~$1M/day) are minted and distributed to subnets based on staking flows. Templar gets 6.86% (highest share) — ~247 TAO/day (~$71K).

This is how miners get paid for GPU compute. There's no revenue, no customers. Everything is funded by minting new tokens.

Two Layers of Dilution

Layer 1: TAO (network level)

3,600 new TAO/day across all subnets. 21M max, ~8.9M circulating. ~13% annual inflation (was 26%+ before Dec '25 halving).

Layer 2: Alpha (subnet level)

Each subnet emits its own token. Templar alpha: 21M max, 4.22M circulating. New alpha goes to miners, validators, and subnet creators. This is the supply growth in the charts.

What Does TAO Flowing Into the Pool Do?
This cuts both ways. The alpha token trades in an AMM pool against TAO.
Bullish: More TAO backing each alpha token

~247 TAO/day flows into the pool reserves. Since alpha price = TAO in pool / alpha in pool, more TAO pushes price up. It's like a daily cash injection. This is why market cap has grown despite constant selling.

Bearish: It's inflation, not real demand

That 247 TAO/day is newly minted — not revenue from customers. If TAO's price drops (because TAO itself is being diluted), the dollar value of the injection drops too. Tokens backing tokens with no external revenue anchor.

The tiebreaker: Staking flows

Emission share is based on net staking inflows. More people stake into Templar = bigger share. The 6.86% isn't fixed — it's the market voting Templar as the most valuable subnet. Works great on the way up, unwinds on the way down.

Covenant-72B: What They Built

On March 10, 2026, Templar finished training a 72-billion-parameter AI model — the largest permissionless decentralized training run in history.

  • No data center. ~20 independent people worldwide, each with 8 NVIDIA B200 GPUs. 70 unique participants total.
  • No whitelist. Anyone with the hardware could join or leave freely.
  • Commodity internet. SparseLoCo compression (146x) made bandwidth a non-issue.

Endorsed by Jensen Huang (NVIDIA CEO), Chamath Palihapitiya, and Jack Clark (Anthropic co-founder).

How Good Is It?
MMLU 67.1 — roughly LLaMA-2-70B (July 2023). ~3 years behind frontier. Beats LLaMA-2 on MATH (26.3 vs 10.7).

The achievement isn't the model quality — it's proving permissionless decentralized training works at this scale. Whether it can close the gap to frontier models is the open question.
